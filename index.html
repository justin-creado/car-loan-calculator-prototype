How Car Loans Work

A car loan lets you buy a vehicle now and pay for it in monthly instalments over time — instead of paying the full amount upfront.

When you apply for a loan, the bank or lender looks at a few key factors to calculate your monthly payment:

  • Car price — the total cost of the vehicle you want to buy
  • Down payment — the amount you pay upfront; the more you put down, the less you borrow
  • Interest rate — the cost of borrowing money, shown as a yearly percentage
  • Loan tenure — how long you take to repay the loan (usually 1–5 years)

A higher down payment or shorter tenure means lower total interest. A longer tenure means smaller monthly payments, but you pay more overall.

Eligibility Criteria

Requirement Typical Expectation Why It Matters
Minimum Age 21 years or older Lenders require borrowers to be financially and legally responsible
Minimum Salary AED 5,000/month for new cars; AED 3,000/month for used cars Ensures you can comfortably cover monthly repayments
Credit History Good credit score preferred A clean repayment record improves your chances of approval and better rates
Employment Stability Steady employment or verifiable business income Shows lenders you have a reliable source of income
Existing Financial Commitments Total debts should not exceed 50% of your monthly salary Lenders check you're not over-borrowed before approving more credit
